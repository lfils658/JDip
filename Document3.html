    It's still happening - more Shark Attacks. This time it occurred in California and was a very bloody affair, not to mention deadly. Show/Hide the news article for the details.

    Fatal Shark Attack off Calif. Coast
     
    Mendocino, CA, Aug. 17 (UPI) -- A shark attacked and killed a diver exploring abalone beds in waters 15 to 20 feet deep off the California coast near Mendocino.
     
    Cliff Zimmerman watched from about 3 feet away as a dorsal fin raced by and his diving partner, Randy Fry, disappeared in a cloud of blood 150 feet from shore Sunday, the San Francisco Chronicle reported Tuesday. "I heard a noise, like 'whoosh,' like a submarine, like a boat going by fast. It was a shark. I knew it was a shark. It almost brushed me. I saw its dorsal fin. I don't know what kind it was; all I know is, it was big. Big. It was big enough to kill," Zimmerman said. "It was massive. I was yelling and yelling, but I knew from the amount of blood that it was fatal. He came in for the kill."
     
    An extensive search for Fry was launched, but was called off at sundown Sunday. Fry's remains were eventually discovered late Monday morning. According to the Mendocino County Coroner, Fry died of a large bite to his torso. He is believed to be the victim of a Great White shark.
